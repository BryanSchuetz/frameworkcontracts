5 Senior Experts in Agro-economy, Agriculture Production Systems, Value Chains, Irrigation Development and Agricultural Finance

  • Project: Formulation of the 11th EDF Sustainable Agriculture Programme for Malawi (KULIMA)
  • Lot: BENEF 1
  • Assignment Duration: 210 working days (total)
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: 2016/373295
  • Location: Malawi
  • Estimated Start Date: 5th April 2016
  • Application Deadline: 4th March 2016

The global objective of this assignment is to formulate a result and impact oriented sustainable agriculture programme that will support the Government of Malawi (GoM) to enhance food, nutrition and income security.

The objectiveof this consultancy assignment is to provide decision makers in the Government of Malawi and European Commission with sufficient information and analysis to justify the choice of areas and implementation modalities for the KULIMA as a sustainable agriculture programme of the 11th European Development Fund (EDF) for Malawi. In this frame, the overall outcome of the consultancy will be: to provide adequate information which will constitute the basis for concluding a financing agreement between the EU Commission and the GoM to undertake the ambitious transformative sustainable agriculture programme. This will involve the appraisal and strengthening of the design, coherence, relevance, feasibility and potential for effectiveness of a draft action document, along with the elaboration of implementation modalities and the necessary stakeholder collaboration and accountability and coordination arrangements for the implementation of the programme.

The specific objectives of the assignment are to provide advise so that:

  • The choice of areas and implementing modalities proposed in the programme will have a broadbased impact in transforming smallholder homestead diversified farming, increases agriculture production and productivity, contributes to overall improving the nutritional status of Malawians and strengthens value chain development and promotes value addition, ensures easy access to markets and generation of increased incomes for rural households, promotes skills to adapt/mitigate climate change effects, and sustainable management of natural resources in the agriculture sector;
  • Adequate conditions under which EU Aid will have a structural impact in transforming multisector policy and planning, interaction, collaboration and joint operations in addressing food, nutrition and income security in Malawi;
  • The programme design will provide the possibility for EU Member States to join and/or cooperate within an agreed programme, as well as to integrate continental and regional recommendations and country-level concerns and considerations;
  • The programme design will define means and strategies for effective diversification of agriculture production in Malawi;
  • The programme design will ensure that the county / district dimension is taken into consideration.

5 Senior Experts in Agro-economy, Agriculture Production Systems, Value Chains, Irrigation Development and Agricultural Finance

A formulation team of indicatively 5 experts of category I is expected for a total of approximately 210 working days (for both phase 1 and 2).

The proposed Team Leader and key experts covering agroeconomy, agriculture production systems, agriculture value chain development, irrigation development and agricultural finance are preferably to be covered by a category I expert. A combination of expertise can be covered by one expert.

The team is required to have the following general expertise:

  • Team leader with strong ability to lead, coordinate and manage a team to provide high-quality inputs. This requires proven managerial, project management and team coordination skills. (S)He must have been team leader of at least one previous EDF formulation mission for the EU;
  • Up to date knowledge of latest developments in the fields related to the programme, including innovative approaches;
  • All members of the team must have excellent inter-personal and communication skills, including the ability to work with and adjust to diverse audiences (such as politicians, Government technical staff, development partners, civil society, private sector, local chiefs, and smallholder farmers);
  • Within the team there should be knowledge and expertise in organisational capacity development, governance and change management in low-income countries, particularly in relation to agricultural transformation;
  • At least one team member should have experience of working with multi-sectoral programmes, with Governments (at national and sub-national levels, urban and rural), Non-State Actors (e.g. academia, research institutes, private sector including micro-enterprises, farmer organisations, CBOs/CSOs/NGOs; financial institutions) and Development Partners;
  • Within the team there should be expertise in M&E and reporting systems following Project Cycle Management (PCM) logic;
  • At least one team member must be familiar with international policies related to the issues covered by the programme, as well as with EU development policy and EC development cooperation programmes and EU contractual procedures;
  • Experience of similar assignment(s) in Sub-Saharan Africa is an asset.

The formulation team will need the following KULIMA specific expertise:

  • Senior Agro-economist with familiarity of practical participatory approaches.
  • Expertise in extension systems with specialist knowledge and experience in design and implementation of the Farmer Field schools (FFS) approach and farmer Business School (FBS) approach and with experience in support of farmer-led research;
  • Expertise in development of irrigation systems in sub-saharan Africa;
  • Expertise in practical use of integrated production methods (ICM/IPM practices, agroforestry etc…) under tropical and sub-tropical conditions;
  • Expertise on different types of cooperative and farmers' organization development as well as anchor farms and contract farming modalities, preferably in African countries;
  • Thorough knowledge of private sector in agriculture with focused expertise in agriculture value chain development and promotion of micro-, small and medium sized enterprises;
  • Expertise in agriculture related financial service delivery, including formal banking system and other types of financial service providers (e.g. micro-finance, warehouse receipt systems, credit schemes, Village Savings and Loans etc.);
  • Expertise on cross-cutting issues, most particularly nutrition, gender, human rights, social protection and governance must be present in the team.
  • Expertise on trade related issues (domestic and regional) is an asset;
  • Expertise on food safety and food technology, quality assurance, standardization, accreditation and metrology requirements is an asset.

Language and IT skills:

• All team members must have excellent skills in spoken and written English; • All team members must be fully computer-literate.

Education:

Senior Experts (Team Leader and key experts) should have at least a Master’s Degree or equivalent in a discipline relevant to the required expertise of this ToR and to their assignment in the team and 12 years of general professional experience.

Extra years of experience would be acceptable as an alternative to a Master’s Degree. A senior expert (category I) without a Master’s Degree would require relevant professional experience of at least 15 years;
