Capacity Building Adviser, Labour Mobility Initiative

  • Project: Labour Mobility Initiative (Pacific)
  • Lot: DFAT
  • Assignment Duration: upto 34 months
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: AAP1314/AAS-4
  • Location: Home-based; travel to the Pacific region
  • Estimated Start Date: tba
  • Application Deadline: 12 November 2014

Since 2009 Australia has funded Pacific island governments under the Labour Mobility Initiative (LMI) to recruit, prepare and manage workers for job placements in the Pilot Seasonal Worker Pilot Scheme (PSWPS) and then the Seasonal Worker Program (SWP).

In September 2013, a design mission was undertaken to develop a new phase of the LMI with a focus on improving the capacity of participating governments to recruit, prepare and manage workers for the SWP and to assess new options for capacity development and delivery. Any capacity building assistance would continue to focus on Pacific island governments as managers of the recruitment process.

The new LMI aims to improve the capacity of participating governments to manage aspects of the SWP. There are nine participating countries in the SWP – Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, Papua New Guinea, Kiribati, Tuvalu, Nauru, Samoa, Tonga and Timor-Leste.

The overall goal of the LMI is to address poverty through increasing incomes, improving human capital and maximising the use of earnings from labour mobility for women and men in the region. At the conclusion of the LMI, there are expected to be four primary outcomes:

  • Increased numbers of workers from participating countries take up seasonal work opportunities
  • Communities benefiting from the effective use of remittances earned through labour mobility
  • Women and men participating in the seasonal worker program have increased employability
  • Increased partner government capacity to manage international labour

The Capacity Building Adviser will have responsibility for designing and supporting a program of capacity building assistance to participating government agencies and other stakeholders, ensuring all services are delivered to best-practice standards, in accordance with the requirements and terms of the contract, and for managing the day-to-day activities of other technical advisers as appropriate.

Mandatory competencies and experience

  • Extensive experience working in developing countries.
  • Proven capacity to build and maintain effective relationships with senior personnel in government and donor agencies.
  • Comprehensive knowledge of contemporary human resource development strategies, planning and implementation, and the conduct and analysis of training needs assessments/analysis.
  • Ability to design and deliver training courses, development of culturally relevant materials and evaluate the effectiveness of training and capacity building.
  • Proven ability to deliver a suite of development activities in a developing country.
  • An understanding of the social, cultural, political and institutional factors affecting development in the Pacific and Timor-Leste
  • Strong analytical skills, strong interpersonal, oral and written communication skills in English.

Desirable competencies and experience

  • Tertiary qualification in a relevant field.
  • Experience in the education, training and human development sectors in the Pacific region.

The Capacity Building Adviser may have up to 34 months of inputs over the years of the LMI, with the first 28 months being full time from the Services Order start date, with 6 months allocated across the final 12 months (in the event that the Option to Extend was enacted).

If you meet the criteria and would like to be considered for this nominated role please email your CV with a covering note outlining how you fit the requirements. Please email your application to sarah_owen@dai.com quoting LMI/CBA in the subject line of your email.

Only shortlisted candidates will be contacted.
