Four Senior Experts, Formulation Mission for a Sector Reform Contract in JLOS and the Accountability Sector in Uganda

  • Project: Formulation Mission for a Sector Reform Contract in JLOS and the Accountability Sector in Uganda under 11th EDF
  • Lot: BENEF 7
  • Assignment Duration: 70 days
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: 2017/383823 - Version 1
  • Location: Uganda
  • Estimated Start Date: 13th March 2017
  • Application Deadline: 10th February 2017 (12.00 noon GMT)

The global objective of this assignment is to assist the European Union Delegation in Uganda in the formulation of a Sector Reform Contract in the area of governance under the 11th European Development Fund.

The specific objective of this assignment is to provide all required elements for the formulation of a Sector Reform Contract in a sector to be confirmed by QSG-1 (provisionally JLOS and the Accountability Sector) in line with the EU budget support guidelines. The mission outputs will enable the EU Delegation to complete all required documents for a 2017 Financing Decision under the 11th EDF.

The specific objectives of the assignment are:

  • Sector study:
  • analyse the eligibility conditions for sector budget support in line with the EC’s guidelines: 1) the country has an adequate, stability-oriented macroeconomic framework, 2) there is a credible and relevant programme to improve public financial management, 3) sufficient level of budgetary transparency and 4) a credible and relevant sector policy with an appropriate monitoring framework;

  • Identify weaknesses and sector priorities where the EU programme could contribute to progress, taking into account the division of labour and EC’s strategic priorities

  • Programme identification: consider the findings of the sector study and in consultation with different stakeholders identify the programme.

  • Programme formulation: in consultation with decision makers provide all required elements for the finalisation of the action document and build a common understanding of the functioning of the SRC and the necessary coordination mechanism to be established.

4 Senior Experts (cat. I)

The team must collectively offer the following expertise and experience:

Qualification and skills:

  • At least an advanced university degree in public financial management, macroeconomics, public administration or other relevant fields. Qualification in the fields of public financial management, a qualification as a Chartered Accountant or Chartered Management Accountant will be accepted as an alternative to Master’s degree.
  • At least an advanced university degree in law. Expertise in JLOS performance management and monitoring systems and structures in support of achieving development objectives. Knowledge of the systems used in Uganda will be an added advantage;
  • Expertise in PFM disciplines, including (but not limited to) public finance management, accountancy, budgeting, performance monitoring, internal control, auditing and PFM capacity building, developing and implementing comprehensive PFM improvement and reform initiatives in a decentralized setting;
  • Development expertise, including knowledge of development priorities and challenges. Knowledge of accountability and anti-corruption policies, strategies and programmes specific to Uganda will be advantageous.
  • Knowledge of MDGs - SDGs and EU action plans and programmes in support of them. Proven experience in gender targeting and mainstreaming in programme formulation.
  • Expertise in public service capacity development and reform initiatives and programmes. Recent experience of working in or with the Ugandan public service will be advantageous.

Minimum expertise required:

  • At least 5, preferably 10 years' experience in supporting and advising governments in PFM reforms. Experience in Uganda and /or in federal and decentralised contexts will be an asset.
  • Experience in developing PFM and budget transparency annual reporting mechanisms and in related policy dialogue on PFM.
  • At least 5, preferably 10 years' experience in supporting and advising governments in JLOS and anti-corruption. Experience with EU budget support, preferably in the JLOS and PFM sectors, will be an asset.
  • Good understanding and knowledge of various models to finance the JLOS and PFM sectors and/or in national and decentralised contexts.
  • Proven experience in developing budget support in the JLOS and PFM sectors in national and decentralised contexts.
  • Proven experience with gender sensitive budgeting, and its operationalisation such as through gender compacts and/or similar approaches
  • Proven experience in analysing gender-equality, the rights-based approach, environment and climate change as cross-cutting issues in EU-financed development programmes and in mainstreaming these issues into programme formulation.
  • At least 5, preferably 10 years' experience with formulating EU budget support and proven experience in training senior government officials on EU budget support.
  • Recent working experience in conducting reviews of the key (eligibility) areas of assessment in decentralised governance structure for budget support by the EU.
  • Recent EU programming expertise covering financing modalities including BS, SBS (SRC), and Pool Funding in the context of EC General Budget procedures. Experience with SRCs in the area of governance is considered an asset.
  • Knowledge of EC management modes including but not limited to centralised and decentralised management, is also required.
  • All experts should be fluent in spoken and written English, and should have excellent technical writing skills.
