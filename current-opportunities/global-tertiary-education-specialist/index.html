Global Tertiary Education Specialist

  • Project: Regional Advisory Services (RAS)
  • Lot: DFAT
  • Assignment Duration: 60 days’ input over two years
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: 7014046
  • Location: Suva, Fiji / Pacific Region
  • Estimated Start Date: ongoing project
  • Application Deadline: 12 August 2014

Australia has established the Regional Advisory Service (RAS) to provide regional organisations, their island members and the DFAT Pacific regional program with timely and efficient access to a small pool of experts to support. Beyond the use of individual advisers, this integrated approach to structuring their use of such expertise will improve coordination and networking amongst regional organisations, and with national priorities in member states. It will also more readily enable common themes, challenges and synergies to be identified and addressed in a coherent and efficient way.

The RAS will offer the following:

  • A vehicle for joint analysis and learning on the major issues and challenges identified through regional organisation governing bodies, policy dialogue and elsewhere.
  • Access to a pool of experts (or other inputs and activities) that can be mobilised to work with one or across a number of regional organisations, with member states and with the Australian government or jointly in the following areas: strategic planning, governance, monitoring and evaluation, audit and risk management.
  • A help desk function, where technical staff are available remotely throughout the year for up to 20 days per year. Typical tasks include specific technical advice, reviewing technical documents produced and joint-assessment of technical issues. This function should only be used where the staff of regional organisations cannot address a problem themselves and would be moderated through the joint focal points in DFAT and the regional organisations.

DFAT will manage the RAS Team through the Suva hub as part of an enhanced engagement on Pacific Islands Regionalism. DFAT will work jointly with regional organisations and member states to determine their priorities for support including through High Level Consultations and regular discussions between focal points.

Global Tertiary Education Specialist

Essential Requirement:

Relevant qualification in international development, international relations or related discipline, and over 20 years’ experience in public sector policy, strategy and program management in development contexts.

Essential Occupational Experience:

  • Extensive knowledge of and a minimum of 20 years’ experience in international development;
  • Comprehensive knowledge of and experience with DFAT’s programming and corporate policies and operations;
  • Extensive experience working in development in the Pacific region;
  • Extensive knowledge of benchmarking, strategic planning and capacity building for establishing and improving tertiary education institutions

Highly desirable:

Experience of working with Pacific regional educational institutions.

The specialist is required for approximately 60 days’ input over two years.

If you meet the criteria and would like to be considered for this role please email your CV in DFAT format with a covering note outlining how you fit the requirements. Please email your application to sarah_owen@dai.com quoting reference no. 7014046 in the subject line of your email.

Only shortlisted candidates will be contacted.
