Mid-term evaluation MDTF Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, FATA and Balochistan 2016 and 2017

This assignment has as objective to contribute to the Annual Review of the MDTF 2016 and 2017. The overall objective of MDTF is to support the implementation of a programme reconstruction and development aimed at facilitating the recovery of the affected regions in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province (“KP”), the Federally Administered Tribal Areas (“FATA”) and the Province of Baluchistan (“Baluchistan”) from the impact of: (i) the armed conflict and reducing the potential for escalation or resumption, and (ii) the floods of 2010.

Under the overall coordination of the Team Leader and in close cooperation with the other members of the AR evaluation team, the expert is expected to contribute to the overall review conducted by a team of experts specialised in complementary fields and provide quality inputs related to his expertise in the part “Monitoring and evaluation – results” and other areas as assigned. Key work stream will include:

  • How effective are the mechanisms in place for monitoring and evaluation and is the data required adequate, available in the format required and sufficiently robust.
  • Quality assure the output data available for the review and provide recommendations, as appropriate.
  • Analyse if the programme data sufficiently reflect its focus on poor and underserved areas, women, girls and children, and the requirements of the Gender Equality Act?

Project Duration: January 2017 - January 2018

Key expert C II: Monitoring and Evaluation – Results, 20 w/d

Qualifications and skills

  • Masters Degree in in business management or administration, development studies, economics, social science or any other relevant field or its absence, equivalent professional experience.
  • Strong skills in writing clear, well structured and concise reports.

General professional experience

  • A minimum of 6 years of experience in program management of programs in the governance sector

Specific professional experience

  • At least 3 years proven professional experience in monitoring and evaluation of development programs
  • At least 3 years professional experience in project cycle management and well versed in the use of the logical framework
  • Knowledge of recent developments in programme/project M&E;
  • Experience in evaluating complex development project/programmes, including in the fields of local or rural development and/or early recovery and rehabilitation desired
  • Experience in evaluation of Multi-donor programs, preferably Multi Donor Trust Funds and/or World Bank administered programs desired
  • Experience in evaluating projects using the DFID methodology and templates highly desired
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills and be able to work in a team

Language:

Excellent knowledge of English, spoken and written is a must, Urdu is an advantage.

The expert must be independent and free from conflicts of interest in the responsibilities he/she takes on.
