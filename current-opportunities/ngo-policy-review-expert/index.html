External Review of the National NGO Policy of the Republic of Botswana

Global objective

The overall objective is to contribute to enhancing the partnership between Government and CSOs to achieve national development goals as set in the NDP11 and Vision 2036. The aim is to develop a more strategic and accountable framework for CSOs â€“ Government relations, characterised by an effective participation of CSOs in the design, implementation, monitoring and evaluation of development policy, while safeguarding independence and autonomy of CSOs.

Specific objective(s)

More specifically, the purpose of the assignment is to undertake an external assessment of the Botswana National NGO Policy and provide inputs into its review. According to the same NGO Policy, the review should take place after five years of implementation, that is April 2018.

The NGO Policy review is fundamental to develop a long-term strategic framework to strengthening civil society and promote effective and accountable partnership between CSOs and Government, aligning and integrating it with NDP11 and Vision 2036. The review should allow the NGO Policy to be updated and adapted to the changed context and emerging priorities of civil society. In particular, the review of the NGO Policy shall be informed by the findings and recommendations of an external assessment of the implementation of the past five years, looking in particular at the following aspects:

  • The relevance of the strategic framework of the NGO Policy to the evolving context of civil society and CSOs - government relations, the NDP11 and Vision 2036;
  • The adequacy of the current framework for civil society participation and engagement in policy dialogue, looking at the experiences of the Technical Working Groups and the Stakeholders Forum, in view of improving spaces and mechanism of dialogue, integrating effective CSOs participation into the national development planning system
  • The legitimacy and credibility of CSOs and the capacity of the CSO networks to enhance and channel the voice of citizens and disadvantaged groups;
  • The institutional set up of the NGO Policy, identifying the adequate parent ministry and linkages to relevant LMs to promote the goals of the policy;
  • The implementation mechanisms and coordination of the NGO Policy in view of making them more strategic, practical, effective and accountable;
  • The monitoring and evaluation system for the NGO Policy.

NGO Policy Review Expert

Minimum requirements:

  • Master’s Degree in law, international law, public policy, political sciences or other relevant field OR in the absence of a formal Master’s Degree in the required field: relevant work experience of at least 10 years;
  • At least 6 years of professional experience in the area of strengthening of civil society.

Specific requirements:

  • Participation in at least 3 evaluations, concerning national civil society assessments and/or review of policy relevant to CSOs; Proven professional experience in design of CSO policy frameworks;
  • Extensive knowledge of NGO policy international best practices and wide expertise on issues related to CSOs, policy dialogue and enabling environment as demonstrated by relevant research, publications and reports;
  • Demonstrable working experience in CSO sectors in Africa (experience in countries of Southern Africa shall be considered an added advantage);
  • Fluency in oral and written English is compulsory.

All experts must be independent and free from conflicts of interest in the responsibilities they take on.
