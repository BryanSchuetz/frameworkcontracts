Nutrition Experts with EU and African Experience and Expertise in Geospatial Mapping, M&E, Reviews/Assessments/Reforms

  • Project: Nutrition Mapping and 11th European Development Fund (EDF) Identification Study for Malawi
  • Lot: BENEF 1
  • Assignment Duration: 13 weeks
  • Eligible Nationalities: all
  • Project Reference: 2014/351087 - Version 1
  • Location: Malawi (Lilongwe and districts)
  • Estimated Start Date: 17 November 2014
  • Application Deadline: 16 October 2014

The specific objectives of this consultancy assignment are:

  • To support the development of a national inventory of existing nutrition interventions in Malawi;
  • To support the EDF National Authorising Office (NAO) and the European Union Delegation to the Republic of Malawi (EUD) in identifying relevant 11th EDF support to the nutrition sector in Malawi.

Three Nutrition Experts with EU and African Experience and Expertise in Geospatial Mapping, M&E, Reviews/Assessments/Reforms Team Leader (Category I) (43 working days) Two Category II Experts (36 working days):

  • Master’s Degree or equivalent in nutrition or in a discipline relevant to the nutrition sector. Extra years of experience would be acceptable instead of a Master’s Degree. A senior expert (category I) without a Master’s degree would require relevant professional experience of minimum 15 years;
  • extensive knowledge and experience in nutrition sector related issues, including successful experience of at least (for the TL) 10 years in sector reviews/assessments, sector reforms and preparation of programmes, with at least 5 years within the Sub-Saharan African region;
  • should be able to demonstrate strong analytical skills; experience in conducting geospatial mapping and analysing Geographical Information Systems (GIS) data is an advantage;
  • strong analytical skills of developments in the context of sectors related to nutrition (e.g. food security, agriculture, epidemiology, health, sanitation etc.);
  • comparative country experience in formal and non-formal nutrition sub-sectors, particularly experience in crosscutting Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary Level education; nutrition service delivery (frontline staff training) and research development in sub-sector analysis, including analysis and measures to strengthen the links between professional skills development, evidence based information generation and the market and to promote involvement of the private sector and Non State Actors (NSAs) in the sector;
  • sound monitoring and evaluation, data analysis and/or statistical analysis expertise;
  • extensive knowledge of applied information technology, including database design and management (preferably linked to GIS and web-based applications), coupled with comparative country experience in organisational dynamics at public sector level, preferably in Sub-Saharan Africa;
  • sound knowledge of EU procedures and aid modalities;
  • must have comparative country experience in successfully mainstreaming cross cutting issues such as gender and environment. Additional expertise on HIV/AIDS, climate change, human rights and good governance is an added advantage.
  • The Team Leader must have sound management skills and must demonstrate that (s)he has successfully managed a team of minimum 3 people for at least 2 years.
  • fully computer literate, with good personal and communication skills; excellent skills in spoken and written English. Knowledge of Chichewa is an advantage.

If you meet the requirements and would be interested in applying for this role please email your CV in EC format to Francesca_Giordano@dai.com

When submitting your tailored CV, please highlight how you answers to each of the above specific requirements.
