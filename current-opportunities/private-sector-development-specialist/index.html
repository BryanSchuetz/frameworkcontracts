Private Sector Development Specialist

  • Project: Building Business Support Service Networks in Pacific Island Countries
  • Lot: MFAT
  • Assignment Duration: tba
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: BBSSN-PIC-1
  • Location: Home-based, with some travel to the Pacific region
  • Estimated Start Date: March 2015
  • Application Deadline: 30 January 2015

The initiative will aim to design and deliver business support service networks in Cook Islands, Fiji, Papua New Guinea, Samoa, Solomon Islands, Tonga and/or Vanuatu. It is anticipated that the activity may be piloted in a sub-set of three to four of these countries initially, with a view to expanding more widely to other countries once established and if successful. The overall goal of this initiative is to increase the growth and prosperity of PIC small to medium enterprises (SMEs) while developing the market for sustainable, commercial business support services.

The initiative will build on previous and existing support programmes and is expected to be completed in two phases. Phase one includes the design of the initiative based on an MFAT concept note, desktop research and consultation with regional and PIC stakeholders. Timing is an important consideration, as it is expected that the design phase will be completed by June 2015.

Phase Two includes the implementation of the initiative. While the scope of services delivered under Phase Two will be determined during Phase One, it is anticipated to include the following tasks: establishment of business support service hubs in selected PICs; establishment and coordination of linkages with regional and in-country business support service providers; development of local capability to expand or maintain support networks and services, and; short-term delivery of business support services as required.

DAI is submitting an Expression of Interest for this project and is looking to identify a Private Sector Development Specialist to nominate in their bid:

Required

  • Knowledge and practical experience of designing/implementing sector policy support programmes in private sector development;
  • Knowledge and practical experience of conducting trade & business sector analysis and assessment, with particular reference to policy, coordination, institutional setting and capacity, and performance monitoring;
  • In-depth and recent understanding of the private sector in the Pacific;
  • Previous experience of working with MFAT would be a strong asset.
  • Fluency in spoken and written English, and excellent writing skills.

Key qualifications and experience:

  • Advanced University degree (master’s degree or equivalent) in a relevant field;
  • Minimum 10 years’ experience of supporting private sector development in developing countries, and relating to the development of SMEs;
  • Extensive experience of working on private sector development involving donor coordination, incorporating recent experience of aid effectiveness, harmonisation and alignment issues in programme design;
  • Previous experience of working in private sector development across the Pacific is required;
  • Excellent inter-personal and communication skills in English language is essential;
  • Computer literacy and public presentation and advocacy skills.

If you meet the criteria and would like to be considered for this nominated role please email your CV with a covering note outlining how you fit the requirements. Please email your application to sarah_owen@dai.com quoting Business Support Service in the subject line of your email.

Only shortlisted candidates will be contacted.
