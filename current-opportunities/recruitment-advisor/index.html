Recruitment Advisor

  • Project: PNG-APP Phase IV
  • Lot: DFAT
  • Assignment Duration: 15 months
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: DFAT/PNG-APP Phase IV
  • Location: Papua New Guinea, Port Moresby and Lae
  • Estimated Start Date: 30 March 2015
  • Application Deadline: 16 February 2015

Program Overview:

Australia is providing support to the Royal Papua New Guinea Constabulary (RPNGC) in accordance with direction from the Australian Government. PNG-APP Phase IV is the deployment of 50 AFP sworn and support staff into Papua New Guinea. These personnel work as Advisors at urban and district level police stations of the RPNGC) in Port Moresby (POM) and Lae, augmenting the previous PNG-APP mission of 17 personnel. PNG-APP personnel also work as inline advisors to the RPNGC Office of Reform. This assistance is an important element in the shared goals of the Government of Australia (GoA) and the Government of Papua New Guinea (GoPNG).

The RPNGC is the national police force with a staffing level of approximately 5200 officers posted around the country. Its mandate is to preserve peace and good order and impartially enforce the law in partnership with the community. Policing in the nation is high on the Government agenda and RPNGC is tasked by GoPNG with managing a very significant program of expansion over the medium term. These reforms are managed and coordinated by the Assistant Commissioner of Police (ACP) Office of Reform.

PNG-APP is seeking to identify advisors with the relevant qualifications and experience to support RPNGC in core areas that support operational policing. The objective of the roles is to work, at all times, with counterparts to build their skills and competencies, and the capabilities of the division, to do the job the RPNGC expects of them.

Objectives of the role:

The recruitment advisor will work closely with the Assistant Commissioner Police, Human Resources, the RPNGC recruitment team and members of the PNG-APP to design, implement and manage RPNGC talent acquisition for base level recruits in accord with the recommendations of the recent review.

Utilising experience and knowledge the advisor will help develop, shape and deliver an appropriate ‘new solution’. They will work with the RPNGC recruitment team and other stakeholders, building strong relationships and partnering with them through the recruitment rebuilding process to:

  • Improve the population of RPNGC talent pools;
  • Better screen and manage the police recruit selection process;
  • Using appropriate recruitment and selection methodologies and tools and outsourcing agreed elements of the selection process;
  • Supporting and educating RPNGC personnel in recruitment better practices and procedures;
  • Monitoring and reporting on operational performance and compliance, capturing relevant data and ensuring stakeholders and management receive timely updates.

Qualifications and Skills:

  • A tertiary degree in a relevant field
  • Work experience in the PNG public service or similar developing country environment

General Professional Experience:

  • Ability to advise on strategic planning, results based management and reporting
  • Ability to lead recruitment, contracts management, performance appraisal, career development management, build teams, maintain high staff morale
  • Strong IT skills
  • Demonstrated ability to work effectively with others to build productive coalitions and contribute to sustainable outcomes, including advocacy and the capacity to negotiate effective solutions
  • High level interpersonal skills including verbal and written communication skills and an exceptional ability to develop and maintain results-focused stakeholder relationships.
  • Ability to implement new systems (business side) and affect staff behavioural/ attitudinal change

Specific Professional Experience:

  • Demonstrated experience in change management and in mentoring counterparts and senior managers through organizational change.
  • Able to partner closely with counterparts and be viewed as a trusted adviser providing strategic business advice enabling solutions whilst also managing the operational aspects of the work being performed.
  • Knowledge of, or the ability to quickly acquire knowledge of, the provincial approach to government across PNG and in particular, the autonomy arrangements of Bougainville, and the law and justice issues that impact on Bougainville.

If you are interested in the position and your expertise/experience matches the terms of reference please email your CV with a brief covering email to Sarah_Owen@dai.com
