Programme Director - Turks and Caicos Islands Business Transformation Programme

Overall objective

The overall objective of the project of which this contract will be a part is to bring about sustainable and inclusive economic growth that will raise living standards and provide more jobs for Islanders and other residents of the Turks and Caicos Islands.

Purpose The purpose of this contract is to monitor and to coordinate the implementation of the Turks and Caicos Islands' Business Reform Strategy.

Results to be achieved by the Contractor:

  • The Business Reform Strategy Plan is regularly updated with relevant and credible deliverables, activities; timeline and budget.
  • The implementation of the Business Reform Strategy is on track with the Plan
  • Its performance is monitored through clear objective and measurable indicators. Progress on key elements is assessed on the basis of baselines.

Project description

The Technical assistant, Programme Director, is required to co-ordinate efforts in delivering the Business Reform Strategy and to lead and manage the TCIG Delivery Team. This team will be drawn from across the civil service and may later include individuals seconded from the private sector. The Programme Director will have a watching brief to ensure that specific EU conditions and targets are met and the monitoring requirements are in place. The EU programme is parallel to – but separate from – the implementation of the Delivery Strategy.

The Programme Director will be responsible for implementing the agreed Business Reform Strategy and ensuring that the TCIG meets the Business Reform Strategy targets. The detailed terms of reference for the Programme Director are essentially set out in the revised and updated delivery plan for 2016.

Senior Expert in Business & Change Management (TL)

Qualifications and skills

  • A first degree with concentration in economics, business, organisational development and/or capacity building required.
  • Post-graduate degree (e.g. MBA) or professional management qualification in economics, business, organisational development and capacity building is highly desirable.

General professional experience

  • The expert will have at least 10 years of experience in economics, business, organizational development and/or capacity building. Specific professional experience
  • Experience in Strategic Planning and Team Leadership is essential.
  • Project management skills, training and experience is required.
  • Knowledge of change management and workforce & business re-design.
  • Knowledge of developing and implementing policies and procedures. The expert must be independent and free from conflicts of interest in the responsibilities they take on.
