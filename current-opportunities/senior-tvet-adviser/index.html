Background

DFAT’s new investment in vocational skills is designed to help close the gap between industry demand for, and Solomon Islands' supply of skilled labour. The Skills for Economic Growth program aims to shift Solomon Islands’ current technical vocational training system from a postcolonial supply driven model to a suite of demand driven training that meets the needs of industry and fills critical skills shortages. The program will include support to the Ministry for Education to develop a new national qualifications framework and a gradual shift to competency based training. There will be tangible investments in developing the quality of courses in high need areas such as in construction, trades, tourism, and early childhood. As well as working in the formal employment sector, there will also be a focus on linking short courses to small business development in the informal economy.

Objectives of the role:

Work closely with the management of the Solomon Islands National University (SINU) and the School of Technology and Maritime Studies (STMS) as Senior TVET Adviser and principal counterpart to the Dean of the STMS.

Length of the project: 24 - 48 Months Start Date: October 2015 ARF Level: B3

Qualifications and Skills:

  • A degree in education and training.
  • Senior-level experience in the management of a TVET or equivalent level post-secondary education and training institution.
  • TVET planning and quality based, demand driven training delivery in developing countries, preferably in Pacific Island contexts.
  • Demonstrated experience in labour market analysis and in designing training in response to identified labour market demand.
  • Demonstrated professional commitment to inclusive processes and experience in developing gender and disability sensitive programs and activities.
  • Competency in the use of MS Word, MS Excel, email and the Internet.

General Professional Experience:

  • Experience working in the Pacific, preferably Solomon Islands.
  • Experience in preparing strategic planning documentation and reporting for Government agencies.
  • Knowledge and understanding of Commonwealth Government contracting and procurement policies and procedures.
  • A first aid qualification

Specific Professional Experience:

  • Excellent cross-cultural communication skills, both oral and written.
  • Ability to establish strong working relationships with a range of government and industry stakeholders.
  • Ability to provide leadership and professional direction within a small but relatively complex environment.
  • Ability to develop and maintain professional and industry networks.
  • Ability to deal with politically and culturally sensitive issues.
  • Highly developed problem solving skills.
