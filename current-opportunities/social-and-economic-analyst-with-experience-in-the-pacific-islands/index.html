Project Information

Impact Assessment of the Small Business Enterprise Centre (SBEC) and the Private Sector Support Facility (PSSF)

HTSPE requires consultants with experience of undertaking Impact Assessments to assist with the upcoming evaluation of the Small Business Enterprise Centre (SBEC) and the Private Sector Support Facility (PSSF) in Samoa.

The objectives of the study are to assess:

  • the role and contribution of SBEC Samoa to the economic livelihoods of small and medium size businesses in Samoa
  • the quality of services provided by SBEC Samoa to their clients and stakeholders
  • the operations of SBEC and costs associated with the operations.
  • the sustainability of SBEC operations in the future
  • the role and contribution of the PSSF to the economic livelihoods of businesses in Samoa
  • the quality of services provided by PSSF Secretariat to their clients and stakeholders
  • the operations of PSSF and costs associated with the operations. The assessment should take into account the impact that the redesigned framework has had on the operations of the facility in delivering its mandate.
  • the sustainability of PSSF operations in the future
  • the results from exploration/promotional visits and trade shows overseas attended by applicants through PSSF funding.

Scope:

  • An economic analysis of the impact of SBEC services to small businesses and the contribution of small businesses to the economy
  • A social analysis of the benefits and costs derived to the families and communities from small businesses supported through SBEC
  • An assessment of the role of small businesses in the development of Samoan economy in terms of employment
  • An analysis of the issues faced by small businesses in Samoa and the need for SBEC’s services
  • Developing baseline information to assist SBEC in developing targets for the next 5 years.
  • An assessment of SBEC’s role and services
  • An economic analysis of the impact of PSSF services to the growth of business in Samoa
  • A social analysis of the benefits and costs derived to the families and communities from small businesses supported through PSSF
  • Developing baseline information to assist PSSF in developing targets for the next 5 years.
  • An economic analysis of the results from overseas trips funded by PSSF for applicants to attend exploration /promotional visits and trade shows in relation to the economic benefit derived from local marketing and promotion. An assessment of impact of PSSF activity on other private sector stakeholders in Samoa (e.g government, private sector organizations)
  • An assessment of PSSF’s role and services in the next five to 10 years
  • An assessment of the achievement or otherwise of an enabling environment for the private sector through the PSSF

Location: Home-based and Samoa

Experience & Qualifications

Social and Economic Analyst with experience in the Pacific Islands (2-3 positions)

  • University degree in a relevant field
  • Must be an economic and/or business analyst with at least 5 years’ experience in this field
  • Social analyst with experience and understanding of the Samoan social and cultural contexts
  • Background and experience in assessing organisations and seeking funding options for organisations in the future
  • Experience in the Samoan business environment and understanding government policies on trade, commerce and industry
  • Oral and written communication skills in Samoan would be an advantage, but not required.

The assignment will start in July 2014, for an estimated 40 days (20 days’ field work plus 15 days for the report preparations, with an additional 5 days for comments).
