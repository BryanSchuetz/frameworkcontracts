Team Leader, Law & Justice Sector, Papua New Guinea

  • Project: Support Law and Justice in Papua New Guinea
  • Lot: DFAT
  • Assignment Duration: 3 years
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: n/a
  • Location: Papua New Guinea
  • Estimated Start Date: August 2015
  • Application Deadline: 10 April 2015

Objectives of the role:

The Team Leader will manage and support a team of technical and operational staff to deliver a highly complex and important DFAT investment to support law and justice in Papua New Guinea. The program will build on Australian support to the sector from the Papua New Guinea-Australia Law and Justice Partnership, which was completed in June 2014 and its ensuing Transition Program, which runs from July 2014 to December 2015.

The program will target agencies along with sub-national institutions and stakeholders in Hela, Southern Highlands, Gulf, Western, Oro and Morobe provinces, plus the Autonomous Region of Bougainville (ARB).

The Team Leader will establish and manage relationships with both potential and identified stakeholders, and will facilitate and coordinate collaboration with other development programs being implemented by DFAT and other donors.

Length of the project: 2016 - 2019 Start Date: August 2015 (to support the mobilisation and transition phase)

Criteria:

  • A tertiary degree in a relevant field (e.g. international development, international law);
  • Demonstrated experience in capacity development techniques (related to technical skills) and the ability to use a variety of approaches to increase the skills and service delivery standards of partner agencies, counterparts and team members.
  • High level interpersonal skills including verbal and written communication skills and an ability to produce high quality technical reports, guidance notes and templates.
  • Demonstrated ability to lead teams of professionals and to coach and mentor junior staff members, and work effectively with others to build strong partnerships and contribute to sustainable outcomes.
  • Demonstrated ability to work sensitively in a complex multi-cultural environment and high levels of personal resilience to work successfully in remote and challenging locations.
  • Demonstrated experience in implementing governance programs in decentralised service delivery contexts and familiarity and understanding of working with law and justice agencies and community justice initiatives;
  • Demonstrated experience in leading organisational development and change management processes;
  • Ability to mainstream gender-sensitive programming and anti-corruption initiatives within program design and implementation;
  • Demonstrated capacity to manage a transitional mobilisation phase in which the managing contractor changes;
  • An outstanding history of working with the Australian Aid program and the Department for Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT);
  • Prior experience in Papua New Guinea and the Pacific region.

How to apply: Please send your CV by email to Sarah_Owen@dai.com. Please note that due to high volumes of applications, we cannot guarantee to respond to each applicant.
