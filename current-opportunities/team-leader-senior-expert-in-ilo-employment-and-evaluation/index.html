Framework Contracts

Team Leader – Senior Expert in ILO/Employment and Evaluation

  • Project: Assessment of Commission's external cooperation with partner countries implemented through the International Labour Organisation – ILO
  • Lot: EC COM 1
  • Assignment Duration: 70 working days
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: 2014/351971 - Version 1
  • Location: Home-based
  • Estimated Start Date: 15 January 2015
  • Application Deadline: 5 November 2014

The overall objective of this study is to assess the extent to which the projects implemented through the International Labour Organisation have reached their expected objectives and results, how their performance can be improved and to what extent they have contributed to develop a more strategic approach of EC development cooperation in the field of employment, decent work, social protection, labour migration and other related areas. The results of this overall independent assessment should serve as guidance for the European Commission involved services and will provide them with practical recommendations for follow-up actions.

Project location: Brussels, Geneva and filed visits to countries of implementation of EC-ILO projects in different regions

Team Leader – Senior Expert in ILO/Employment and Evaluation

Qualifications and skills: Postgraduate degree in Economics, Law, Social Sciences or other relevant field. Academic research in employment-related issues is an asset.

Professional experience:

  • Minimum of 15 years professional experience in employment and/or decent work with relevant experience in developing countries.
  • Experience in designing, managing and evaluating development cooperation programmes in their field of specialisation

  • Good knowledge of the institutional settings of the EU and ILO is required.

  • Excellent English drafting skills.

  • Good working level knowledge of French, Spanish and Portuguese will be an asset.

In addition, the team as a whole will need to prove:

  • Considerable knowledge and expertise in the area of external relations and development cooperation;
  • Good knowledge of European Commission objectives, principles, funding rules and procedures for external cooperation programs;
  • Good knowledge of the International Labour Organisations mandate, structure and functioning;
  • Experience in evaluating/assessing projects and programmes, including in the field of employment, decent work and social issues.
  • Thematic knowledge and experience in most of the regions covered by the EC Programmes: Southern Mediterranean and Middle East, Eastern Europe, Southern Caucasus, Central Asia, Asia, ACP-Countries, Latin America.

The working languages of this study will be English and French.

If you meet the requirements and would be interested in applying for this role please email your CV in EC format to Agnese_Ortolani@dai.com
