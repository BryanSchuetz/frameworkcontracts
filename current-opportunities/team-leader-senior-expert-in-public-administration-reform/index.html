Study on Gender Equality in Public Administration in the Western Balkans

The overall objective of this assignment is to contribute to strengthening girl’s and women’s voice and participation in the Western Balkans, in particular, women’s empowerment and expanded participation in policy-making and leadership in the executive branch of the state. The study will cover the Western Balkans (Albania, Bosnia & Herzegovina, Kosovo*, the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia, Montenegro and Serbia) and should achieve the following specific objectives:

  • Provide an overview of the current state-of-play, barriers and challenges to women’s participation in decision-making, both participation in public policy/strategy development and participation in leadership positions. The analysis will focus on the thematic area ‘strengthening voice and participation’ identified in the Gender Action Plan.

  • Analyse country-specific legal frameworks, policies and initiatives indicating major constraints and enabling factors of promoting gender equality and women’s empowerment in public administration.

  • Identify policy and programming recommendations for further action, including specific entry points for the EC to advance women’s equal participation in public administration.

Team leader, Senior Expert in Public Administration Reform (maximum 70 working days)

Education:

Masters' Degree in relevant area such as Public Administration, Public Policy, Social and Political Sciences, Gender Studies, International Law, or equivalent.

General experience:

  • minimum 10 years of relevant professional experience in one or more of the areas of gender, governance (with particular emphasis on public administration reforms), and/or data collection;
  • Experience in project management;
  • Experience coordinating/supervising studies/research projects;
  • Experience with quantitative research and data collection;

Specific professional experience:

Experience in analysis and research on public administration reform and/or gender mainstreaming in the public sector:

  • Extensive knowledge of the international framework on gender equality and women’s empowerment, particularly in the area of women’s participation in policy and decision-making and leadership;
  • Excellent knowledge of decision-making processes in public policy, public service delivery, institutional capacity building, human resource management policies, professional ethics and/or civil service reform would be desirable;
  • Knowledge of the EU Gender Equality Policy and Gender Action Plan would be considered an asset.
  • Previous experience in the Western Balkans in issues related to public administration reform and/or gender equality will be a strong asset.

Language skills:

  • Excellent spoken and written English
  • Knowledge of any of the languages spoken in the Western Balkans would be a distinctive advantage.
