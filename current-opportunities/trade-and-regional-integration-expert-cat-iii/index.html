  • Project: Mid-term evaluation of the Myanmar Trade Development Programme and formulation of Myanmar National component of ARISE Plus
  • Lot: BENEF 10
  • Assignment Duration: 9 months
  • Eligible Nationalities: All
  • Project Reference: 2016/380254 - Version 1
  • Location: Myanmar (mainly Nay Pyi Taw and Yangon)
  • Estimated Start Date: 16 January 2017
  • Application Deadline: 14 November 2016 (12.00 midday UK time)

Mid-term evaluation of the Myanmar Trade Development Programme and formulation of Myanmar National component of ARISE Plus

The global objective of the assignment is to conduct a midterm external evaluation of the “Myanmar Trade Development Programme” and to support the EU Delegation in the identification and formulation of the Myanmar National component of the ARISE Plus Programme under MIP Asia 2014-2020.

The specific objectives of the assignment are to:

  • provide the relevant services of the European Union, the partner government and, when appropriate, the wider public with an overall independent assessment of the past 24 months of the TDP intervention, paying particular attention to the level of results achievements of the project against its objectives (Phase 1 - Mid Term Evaluation);
  • deliver key lessons and recommendations in order to improve TDP action and functional to the identification and formulation of the new EU-funded Aid for Trade programme, and accordingly undertake the analysis and prepare a detailed project design of the Myanmar component of the ARISE Plus programme presented in a draft Action Fiche and other related documents (Phase 2 - Programme Formulation).

Trade and Regional Integration Expert – Cat. III

  • A Master degree or equivalent in economics, international trade, business administration or other relevant social sciences disciplines such as international development and political science with specialisation in international trade and development, or equivalent professional experience of a minimum of 4 years (on top of the below mentioned 3 years' experience).
  • Excellent command of Myanmar and English language, both spoken and written, is essential: this expert must be able to participate in technical discussions, translate, and draft analytical papers in English.
  • Strong inter-personal, communication and networking skills, to support effective liaison and coordination with national counterparts and other stakeholders.
  • Ability to manage a high workload and deliver to tight deadlines.
  • Computer literacy.
  • At least 3 years’ relevant professional experience is essential, preferably in the Trade and Private Sector Development in Myanmar.
  • Experience in coordination and liaison with the Government of Myanmar in the Trade sector is highly relevant.
  • Experience of conducting sector analysis and assessment, preferably in Trade, is highly relevant.
  • Experience in the design of Trade and Regional Integration support programmes will be a strong asset.
  • Good knowledge of WTO and ASEAN Economic Community and status of the implementation of key ASEAN agreements on trade, services and investment will be highly appreciated.

If you meet the requirements and would be interested in applying for this role please email your CV in EC format to Aurelie_Durand@dai.com
